GivePulse

Data Visualization / User Research

Givepulse is a online community that connects NGOs and volunteers. The purpose of this project was to find the hidden patterns and insights of user activities and to help company executives to better understand their users.

Questions

1.How are the current volunteering events going? Any difficulties? How could GP help?
2.Does volunteering have positive impacts on students and communities?
3.Based on Q2,from GP's perspective, how to enhance the experience of organizing/attending volunteering events?

Procedure

To answer these questions, we collected data from various sources, and then performed data analysis and visualization work. Besides, we also met with users and company leaders to understand the current work flow and user need. These notes became strong support for us to explain analysis results.

Outputs

Conclusions of above questions;
Various formats of visualization: Descriptive statistics; Charts; Heat maps; Geo-visualizations.

Health Eating App

Information Architecture

This project was done for the Information Architecture & Design Course.Comparing to the other projects, IA focuses more on the content, taxonomy, information structure and information flow.

In our project, we chose to design a healthy eating iPad app for elder citizens. The app offers nutrition information and intake suggestion based on real research results, and also, it allows users to log in and track their intake records.

Challenges

1. First time to design for mobile platform and for user groups with special requirements;
2. Lack background in health/medicare field, not familiar with metadata and taxonomy.

Success

1. Quickly completed a background research by reading academic research results, discussing with professors and students majoring in health-care field;
2. Collected and summarized design guidelines for elder citizens.
3. Implemented HCI design life cycle, including Competitive Analysis, WAAD, Personas, Wireframes, Prototyping, Heuristic Evaluation, etc

Tools Used

Axure, Balsamiq, Photoshop
